About the framework

What is the Healthcare Fragmentation Index?

The HFI is a tract-level framework for measuring the structural organization of local healthcare environments. It is intended to integrate provider availability, spatial accessibility, geographic distribution, and healthcare navigability into reproducible indicators that can be compared across places and linked to health system outcomes.

Core definition

Healthcare fragmentation refers to the degree to which local healthcare resources are spatially constrained, unevenly reachable, or difficult to navigate as a coherent system of care. In HFI v1.1, fragmentation is operationalized as the reversed standardized accessibility score, so higher values represent greater structural constraint.

What HFI is not

HFI is not a hospital quality score, a replacement for HCAHPS, or a definitive measure of all possible forms of care fragmentation. It is a reproducible spatial measurement framework that makes one important dimension of healthcare fragmentation visible: the organization and navigability of local healthcare resources.

Unit

Census tract-level exposure, allowing neighborhood-scale mapping and linkage to institutional outcomes.

Direction

Higher values indicate greater fragmentation; lower values indicate more coherent and accessible healthcare environments.

Use

Designed for replication, validation, extension, and policy-relevant spatial diagnosis.
